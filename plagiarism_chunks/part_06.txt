Because Elevate is designed for real-world deployment and future mobile application integration, the architecture follows a strict Client-Server model. Heavy machine learning processing and database queries are offloaded to Serverless cloud hosting (like Firebase). This ensures that the end-user only needs a basic smartphone or laptop to access the AI ecosystem. Through our analysis, the core problem was defined by three major industry failures: Static and Rigid Nutritional Calculations: Most existing fitness apps calculate a user's required daily calories using basic mathematical formulas. They completely ignore the user's actual daily physical exertion, meaning a user is instructed to eat the exact same amount of carbohydrates on a heavy weightlifting day as they are on a complete rest day. Disconnected Safety and Planning Systems: While advanced computer vision libraries can now track human posture, they exist only as standalone safety scripts. Users currently have to use one application to view their daily workout plan, and boot up an entirely separate application if they want camera-based posture feedback. Ignorance of Real-World Biometrics: Traditional fitness software assumes the user is always perfectly recovered. They assign heavy, demanding workouts without tracking critical outside recovery metrics such as the user's sleep schedule or daily hydration levels, which actively increases the risk of physical injury and overtraining. 5.2 Phase-II (Analysis Phase) The analysis phase evaluates systemic feasibility, defines data processing methodologies, and validates the mathematical models and algorithms driving the Artificial Intelligence of the Elevate platform. Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) and Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE): The platform utilizes the clinical Mifflin-St Jeor Equation to calculate the baseline energy requirements of a user. For Males: BMR = (10 * Weight in kg) + (6.25 * Height in cm) - (5 * Age in years) + 5 For Females: BMR = (10 * Weight in kg) + (6.25 * Height in cm) - (5 * Age in years) - 161 Total Daily Energy Expenditure is then calculated by multiplying the BMR by the day's training intensity multiplier: TDEE = BMR * Daily_Activity_Multiplier 2. Computer Vision Joint-Angle Calculation: The MediaPipe Pose model extracts 33 distinct 3D body landmarks. Posture correctness is calculated using vector geometry. For any joint B connecting points A and C, vectors BA and BC are computed. The joint angle is derived using the dot product formula: Angle = | arctan2(y3 - y2, x3 - x2) - arctan2(y1 - y2, x1 - x2) | * (180 / PI) 5.2.4 Model Validation and Performance Metrics The generation engines use Extreme Gradient Boosting (XGBoost). The Workout Engine utilizes a Multi-Output Regressor to simultaneously predict Sets, Repressions, and Rest Times. The Meal Engine utilizes a Multi-Target Regressor to divide total daily calories into specific macronutrient profiles.
